# Competitive Analysis Methodology

This document defines the methodology for analyzing competitor performance in AI search engines.

## Core Metrics

### Answer Share
**Definition:** The percentage of AI search responses that mention a brand.

**Formula:** `(Responses mentioning brand / Total responses) × 100%`

**Interpretation:**
- 40%+ = Dominant player
- 20-40% = Strong presence
- 10-20% = Emerging presence
- <10% = Weak or absent

**Why it matters:** Answer Share is the primary competitive metric. It directly measures mindshare in AI search. If a competitor has 35% Answer Share and you have 12%, you know you need to triple your visibility to match them.

### Citation Rate
**Definition:** The percentage of responses that cite a brand's website (with a link).

**Formula:** `(Responses citing brand / Total responses) × 100%`

**Interpretation:**
- Citation Rate > Answer Share = High authority (mentioned AND trusted)
- Citation Rate < Answer Share = Low authority (mentioned but not sourced)
- Citation Rate = 0 = No authoritative content

**Why it matters:** Citations indicate trust. AI engines cite sources they consider authoritative. A competitor with high Citation Rate has content that AI trusts — that's a strategic advantage.

### Mention Position
**Definition:** The average position where a brand first appears in responses (when mentioned).

**Formula:** `Sum of first mention positions / Number of mentions`

**Interpretation:**
- Position 1-2 = Top-of-mind
- Position 3-5 = Considered
- Position 6+ = Afterthought

**Why it matters:** Position indicates prominence. Being mentioned 5th in a list is very different from being mentioned 1st. Users skim AI responses and focus on early mentions.

## Gap Analysis

### Gap Topics
**Definition:** Prompts where competitors are mentioned but your brand is not.

**Identification:**
1. For each prompt, check which brands were mentioned
2. If any competitor mentioned AND your brand not mentioned → gap topic
3. Record the topic, which competitors dominated, and why it matters

**Prioritization:**
- High priority: High-intent topics where you have relevant features
- Medium priority: Adjacent topics where you could expand
- Low priority: Topics outside your core positioning

**Why gap topics matter:** These are the highest-ROI opportunities. Competitors are winning mindshare on these topics, and you're invisible. Creating content targeting these gaps directly improves your competitive position.

## Content Strategy Analysis

### Citation Pattern Analysis
For each competitor, analyze what content gets cited:

**Content Types:**
- Comparison guides ("X vs Y")
- Documentation (product docs, API docs)
- Blog posts (thought leadership, tutorials)
- Changelog (product updates, release notes)
- Case studies (customer stories, use cases)

**Citation Signals:**
- Data and statistics (original research, benchmarks)
- Structure (clear headings, lists, tables)
- Authority (established brand, expert authors)
- Freshness (recent updates, current information)

**Strategic Insights:**
- If competitor A gets cited for comparison guides → they're transparent about trade-offs
- If competitor B gets cited for documentation → they have comprehensive, well-structured docs
- If competitor C gets cited for changelog → they're transparent about product development

## Competitive Positioning

### Strength vs Weakness Matrix

For each competitor, identify:

**Strengths:**
- Topics where they dominate (high Answer Share)
- Content types they excel at (high Citation Rate)
- Positioning advantages (first-mover, established authority)

**Weaknesses:**
- Topics they don't cover (gaps in their content)
- Content types they lack (missing comparison guides, outdated docs)
- Positioning vulnerabilities (slow to adopt trends, enterprise-only)

**Your Opportunities:**
- Competitor weaknesses you can exploit
- Gaps in the market no one is filling
- Differentiation angles (AI-native vs legacy, SMB vs enterprise)

## Optimization Strategy

### Closing the Gap
**Approach:** Target gap topics where competitors dominate and you're absent.

**Tactics:**
1. Create comparison content ("Acme vs [Competitor]")
2. Publish guides on gap topics
3. Optimize existing content for these topics

**Expected Impact:** Direct improvement in Answer Share on targeted topics.

### Leapfrogging
**Approach:** Identify competitor weaknesses and exploit them.

**Tactics:**
1. Cover topics competitors ignore
2. Create content types competitors lack
3. Position against their vulnerabilities

**Expected Impact:** Differentiation and mindshare in underserved areas.

### Matching Strengths
**Approach:** Match competitor strengths to stay competitive.

**Tactics:**
1. If competitor has great docs → improve your docs
2. If competitor publishes comparison guides → publish your own
3. If competitor is transparent about updates → be more transparent

**Expected Impact:** Maintain parity on table-stakes topics.
